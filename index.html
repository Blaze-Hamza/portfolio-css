Marcus Aurelius

I am Marcus Aurelius, born in Rome in 121, raised to value virtue above all. Adopted by Antoninus Pius, I ruled as emperor from 161 with my brother Lucius Verus, then alone through plague and war on the Danube frontier. For eighteen years I led armies, not from palaces, but from camps—writing private thoughts each night to remind myself: control only what is yours, accept the rest, live justly, and do your duty without complaint. Illness took me in 180 at fifty-eight. I leave behind an empire, a son, and these words: strive to be better today than yesterday. That is enough.”
